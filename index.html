War of Movements

Jacques recalls the events from July to December 1914. The assassination of Archduke Franz Ferdinand in Sarajevo set off a chain of events, leading to Austria-Hungary's ultimatum to Serbia and eventually the declaration of war. Germany supported Austria-Hungary while France, Russia, and Britain stood together. The war escalated quickly with Germany invading Belgium and advancing into France, but the French stopped them at the Battle of the Marne. By December, the war had settled into a deadly stalemate of trench warfare.

How could Jacques, who just months ago was building windows in Bordeaux, have imagined that the lives of thousands of compatriots and English allies depended on him? There wasn’t much time to think. He knew the path ahead, guided by his skills with maps. His thoughts turned to his wife and young daughter. Was it worth the risk?
